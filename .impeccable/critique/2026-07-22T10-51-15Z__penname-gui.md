## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**LLM assessment**: No longer reads as AI-generated. The parchment canvas, pill chips, hairline sheet table and single teal accent are a committed, coherent system. Two tells remain: the Open screen's 2x2 grid of four same-sized heading+text cards is the "identical card grid" pattern, and the copy leans on em dashes (13 occurrences in GUI strings) against the house rule.

**Deterministic scan**: Unavailable. `detect.mjs` errored with "bundled detector not found" after a real attempt. Manual pass against the ban list found: no gradients, no glassmorphism/blur, no side-stripe borders, no bold weights (400-500 only). Clean on all absolute bans.

**Visual overlays**: Not applicable. This is a PySide6 desktop app with no URL to inject into.

## Overall Impression

The redesign moved this from generic-Qt to a considered tool. Palette, density and the review table now read as designed rather than defaulted. The biggest remaining gap is not visual, it is **feedback**: the app goes silent during its slowest operation and mumbles when things fail. For a nervous, non-technical user handling donor data, silence is the worst possible response.

## What's Working

1. **The review table.** Pill chips, flat sheet, 52px rows, plain-language confidence ("Very sure" / "Less sure, check this"). It makes the mandatory review step feel like a considered checklist rather than a chore.
2. **The vocabulary.** "Pen name", "key file", "Take pen names off" is a rare case of domain language that a non-technical fundraiser can hold in their head without a glossary.
3. **Restraint.** One teal, no bold, hairline borders. The system says no to itself, which is why it now looks intentional.

## Priority Issues

- **[P1] The app goes silent during the scan.** Loading the language model takes 10-30s on first run and the UI offers one static sentence and a disabled button. Users will assume it has hung and force-quit. **Fix:** indeterminate progress indicator, staged copy ("Reading your document" then "Looking for sensitive values"), and keep the window visibly alive. **Command:** `harden`
- **[P1] No drag-and-drop.** The most natural gesture for "open this file" is unsupported; users must go through a picker. **Fix:** accept file drops on the Open screen with a visible drop target state. **Command:** `craft`
- **[P2] The design system has no error colour.** DESIGN.md bans a second accent, so failures render as grey body text indistinguishable from help text. Genuine tension: an app that must report "this could not be pseudonymized reversibly" needs a way to say so. **Fix:** decide an error treatment within the palette (ink-on-parchment notice with a rule, or permit one restrained red). **Command:** `colorize`
- **[P2] Review does not scale past a small document.** A 500-row CRM export becomes 500 rows of manual ticking with no filter, search, or "untick all of this type". **Fix:** filter field plus per-type bulk actions. **Command:** `craft`
- **[P3] Copy uses em dashes throughout** (13 in GUI strings) against the house rule, and a few labels restate their heading. **Command:** `clarify`

## Persona Red Flags

**Margaret (58, Development Director, non-technical, nervous about AI)**: Clicks "Choose a file", picks a 40-page appeal, and the window sits still for 20 seconds with one grey sentence. She assumes she broke it. If a round-trip error appears, it is grey text she cannot distinguish from instructions, with no "try again". She will not trust the tool with real donor data after that.

**Alex (Operations, processes the quarterly CRM export)**: Drags a 2,000-row CSV at the window: nothing happens, no drop support. Then faces 2,000 review rows with no filter and no bulk untick. No keyboard shortcuts. Abandons for a spreadsheet formula.

**Jordan (First-timer, opens it to "see what it does")**: Lands on Open with no sample file and no empty-state guidance beyond format text. Nothing teaches the four-step loop before committing a real document. No in-app help link.

## Minor Observations

- Open and About are bottom-heavy; content stops halfway and leaves a large empty canvas.
- The four "What Penname looks for" cards are informative but identical in shape and weight, which flattens them into wallpaper.
- Nav numbering (1-2-3 then an unnumbered fourth) is slightly ambiguous about whether step four is optional.
- No visible affordance that the review table's pen-name column is editable until clicked.
- Success states are the same grey as errors.

## Questions to Consider

- What would this feel like if the scan were the most reassuring moment in the app rather than its most silent one?
- Does the review step scale to the file sizes a real CRM export produces, or is it designed for the demo case?
- If DESIGN.md forbids a second colour, how should this app say "something went wrong" with conviction?
